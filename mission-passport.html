Mission Passport

A lightweight transfer record for deciding whether a proven AI-enabled workflow is ready to travel from one Harris business unit to another—and what the receiving team must revalidate locally.

01 · Origin
  • Named user / team
  • Workflow and trigger
  • Local business owner
  • Observed before-state
  • Value hypothesis
  • Human judgment boundary
02 · Local proof
  • After-state evidence
  • Retained behavior
  • Exceptions and workarounds
  • Failure modes
  • Where review mattered
  • What did not work
03 · Trust stamp
  • Data classification
  • System access / permissions
  • Retention assumptions
  • AI authority level
  • Human approval point
  • Monitoring and escalation
04 · Transfer packet
  • Reusable job-to-be-done
  • Required context inputs
  • Decision / transformation step
  • Output and recipient
  • Dependencies
  • Known adaptation needs
05 · Arrival
  • Receiving business owner
  • Local baseline
  • Adaptation work
  • Control revalidation
  • Local outcome
  • Stop / scale decision
06 · Return signal
  • Outcome evidence returned
  • Adaptation notes
  • New failure lesson
  • Reusable improvement
  • Trust change
  • Next candidate destination
Decision rule

A Mission is portable when another team can understand the work, evaluate the evidence, see the trust boundary, estimate the adaptation burden, and make a responsible local decision without rediscovering everything from zero.

Suggested review conversation

ValueWhat changed in the work, and what evidence would make us stop?
AdoptionDid the intended users keep using the new workflow after the initial launch?
TrustWhere can AI propose, where can it execute, and where must a human remain accountable?
TransferWhich parts are truly reusable, and which are specific to language, data, integration, policy, customer, or vertical context?
LearningWhat should the next business unit know before it tries?

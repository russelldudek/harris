Dear ServiceLink Hiring Team,

ServiceLink is not hiring an AI strategist to stand outside the work. The role description calls for someone who can assess readiness, discover practical use cases, build and deploy bounded interventions, teach people to use them, establish everyday governance, measure outcomes, and report directly to the EVP—without becoming shadow R&D or shadow IT.

That combination is unusually close to my career through-line. At Vape-Jet, I lead AI-first operating transformation across production, purchasing, quality, customer success, support, engineering issue flow, ERP/Odoo, Jira, HubSpot, Slack, telemetry, and AI-assisted workflows. The work is not “AI adoption” in the abstract. It is making friction visible, deciding what deserves attention, improving handoffs, creating standard work, teaching new behaviors, and instrumenting whether the change holds.

Through DudeWorth, I have developed AI-readiness and opportunity frameworks spanning value, feasibility, data readiness, governance, adoption burden, reuse, and time-to-value. At Amazon, I built operating cadences, escalation paths, throughput visibility, and standard work in a 24/7 customer-backward environment. At Compunetics, I translated between R&D, engineering, manufacturing, quality, customers, and high-reliability execution. Together, those experiences support the role’s hardest requirement: build trust by shipping useful work while creating a system that makes one person’s capacity compound.

My candidate thesis is to run internal AI enablement like a field-service operation. Capture workflow friction. Triage value, data risk, readiness, adoption burden, and owner strength. Dispatch the smallest useful intervention—Enable, Assist, Automate, Build + Integrate, or Escalate. Verify it in real work. Reroute based on evidence.

I built a companion GitHub Pages candidate site at russelldudek.github.io/harris to make that operating thesis tangible. It connects the candidate vision, interactive AI Dispatch Board, interview brief, 90-day plan, and printable Dispatch Ticket as one coherent example of how I turn an ambiguous mandate into mechanisms people can inspect, challenge, and use.

I recognize the obvious concern: I am currently a Director of Operations, while this is a hands-on individual-contributor role with no direct reports. The concern is fair. The part of leadership I value most, however, is not headcount; it is making work better. I am most effective close to the mechanism—observing, building, translating, teaching, measuring, and iterating. The lack of a team makes the operating system more important, not less.

I would welcome the opportunity to discuss which ServiceLink workflows carry the greatest friction today, where AI usage is already emerging informally, and what the EVP would need to see after twelve months to call this function unequivocally successful.

Carpe diem,
Russell Dudek
Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania

Candidate thesis

Run AI enablement like a field-service operation.

ServiceLink gets the right resource to the right work through scheduling, routing, dispatch, visibility, and feedback. Internal AI enablement should use the same operating logic.

Capture friction → Triage value and trust → Dispatch the smallest useful intervention → Verify in real work → Reroute from evidence.

Hard objection

“You are already a Director of Operations. Will you thrive as a hands-on individual contributor with no direct reports?”

The concern is fair. My answer is that the part of leadership I value most is mechanism-building: observe the workflow, make the problem visible, shape the intervention, connect technical and business people, teach the new standard, instrument the result, and iterate. This role needs leverage before hierarchy; that is a feature, not an inconvenience.

Executive interview questions

Questions that expose the real constraint.

The answers determine where Dispatch should begin—and what it should deliberately not do.

  1. What would make the EVP call this role unequivocally successful after twelve months?
  2. Where is AI already used informally, and where is enthusiasm furthest from reliable adoption?
  3. What approved-tool and data-handling boundaries are clear—and where are they still ambiguous?
  4. When a use case needs more than prompting or low-code automation, where is the boundary with IT and R&D?
  5. Which workflows already generate executive or department-head demand for help?
  6. What has made prior cross-functional change stick here—and what has caused it to fade?

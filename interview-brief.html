Interview thesis brief · Russell Dudek

AI Enablement Lead

The role hypothesis: turn MatchaAI adoption into a compounding learning system across Harris businesses—without centralizing the decisions that belong closest to the work.

1. Mandate summary

Nominal roleAI Enablement Lead, remote.
Actual mandate hypothesisHelp Harris business units convert AI access into adopted workflow change, then make proven local patterns safer and faster to transfer across a decentralized portfolio.
Why nowHarris publicly says it accelerated its AI platform in 2025, supporting businesses with security-first MatchaAI Missions and generative tools. The company also added 19 software businesses in 2025 and continues acquisition-led expansion.
Success shapeMore high-value workflows changed, stronger retained adoption, faster proof-to-pattern transfer, explicit trust boundaries, and credible local outcome evidence.

2. Company moment

Harris operates over 200 businesses across more than twenty industries and describes itself as “decentralized yet thoroughly aligned.” Its values reinforce both local empowerment and shared learning: empowerment at the point of contact, communication and knowledge sharing, accountability, disciplined execution, and understanding reality. The enablement architecture therefore needs to preserve local authority while creating shared rails for discovery, trust, measurement, and reuse.

Hypothesis for discovery: the constraint may no longer be AI access. It may be the uneven path from interest → workflow selection → local proof → trust review → manager sponsorship → retained use → transfer to another business.

3. Operating tensions

Autonomy ↔ reuseAccess ↔ workflow redesignSpeed ↔ trustPlatform leverage ↔ vertical nuanceActivity ↔ realized value
Autonomy vs reuseCentralize the transfer standard and shared learning—not every local implementation decision.
Access vs redesignMeasure changed work and retained behavior, not only licenses, prompts, training completions, or demo counts.
Speed vs trustMake the data class, human authority, failure modes, escalation path, and monitoring visible at the point of adoption.
Platform vs vertical nuanceReuse workflow primitives and implementation learning while allowing language, integrations, policies, controls, and metrics to adapt.

4. White-space thesis

Make local AI wins portable without centralizing local judgment.

Each validated MatchaAI workflow can become a reusable asset when it carries enough context to travel: who owns it, which work changed, what the before-state was, where humans retain authority, what trust controls apply, what value was observed, what dependencies exist, and what a receiving team must revalidate.

5. Operating model: the Mission Passport

OriginNamed user, workflow, business owner, before-state, intended value, and human judgment boundary.
Local proofObserved after-state, retained behavior, exceptions, failure modes, and credible outcome evidence.
Trust stampData class, access, retention assumptions, human authority, monitoring, review, and escalation.
Transfer packetReusable job-to-be-done, trigger, context inputs, decision step, output, dependencies, implementation notes, and known adaptation needs.
ArrivalReceiving owner, local re-baseline, adaptation work, control revalidation, outcome, and learning returned to the shared record.

7. Strongest objection

“You have not led enterprise AI enablement across a 15,000-person, 200-business software group.”

Correct. The response is transferability, not title inflation. The work beneath the role—workflow selection, adoption design, cross-functional translation, governance, escalation, measurement, operating cadence, and knowledge transfer—has been central across Russell’s operating career. The first move at Harris should therefore be discovery and small transfer tests, not a large central program.

8. First 90 days

0–30 · MapInterview corporate/platform/security/group/portfolio/BU stakeholders; shadow workflows; inventory enablement touchpoints, Missions, training, measurement, and friction points.
31–60 · TestRun three small transfer tests with willing origin and receiving teams; create Mission Passports; measure time-to-understand, time-to-adapt, adoption friction, trust burden, and outcome evidence.

10. Executive questions

  1. Where has MatchaAI already changed a recurring workflow, rather than merely adding an AI interaction?

  3. Which decisions are intentionally local, and which AI controls or standards should be common?
  4. What would make leaders say this role created value after twelve months?
  5. Where does enablement break down most often: problem selection, data, change burden, sponsorship, training, governance, or measurement?
  6. What is the strongest internal argument against scaling AI enablement more aggressively, and what evidence would change that view?
  7. How are business units currently recognized for contributing reusable learning to other Harris businesses?
  8. Which MatchaAI adoption signals are trusted today, and which feel incomplete or misleading?

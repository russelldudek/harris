The first quarter should create evidence, trust, and a repeatable operating cadence—not a giant AI transformation program. This is a candidate operating hypothesis for discovery.

DAYS 0–30 · MAP

Observe the real work.

  • Interview the EVP, department heads, workflow owners, users, R&D, and IT.
  • Assess readiness by function, approved tools, data sensitivity, friction, and sponsorship.
  • Inventory current AI use, shadow practices, training needs, policies, and boundaries.
  • Establish intake, route definitions, baselines, and potential champions.
DAYS 31–60 · DISPATCH

Ship bounded quick wins.

  • Select a cross-functional portfolio spanning different dispatch routes.
  • Pair each test with an owner, baseline, human authority, boundary, and proof measure.
  • Deliver role-specific workshops tied to live work.
  • Begin EVP reporting on queue, flow, adoption, blockers, and outcomes.
DAYS 61–90 · REROUTE

Turn wins into a system.

  • Retire, reroute, or iterate weak experiments from evidence.
  • Establish champion cadence, reusable playbooks, and escalation paths.
  • Instrument retained adoption and workflow outcomes.
  • Present next-quarter capacity tradeoffs and stop decisions.

Operating principle

Every use case must leave behind a clearer decision, not just a demo.

By day 90, ServiceLink should have a visible queue, shared routing language, approved-use boundaries, measured workflow wins, and an executive cadence that makes constraints explicit.

Day-90 operating system
Page 2 / 2

The output is a functioning internal AI enablement system that can qualify demand, deliver bounded interventions, govern everyday use, and learn from real work.

01

VISIBLE INTAKE + BACKLOG

Named owners, before-state baselines, value hypotheses, data boundaries, adoption burden, and next decisions.

02

FIVE DISPATCH ROUTES

Enable, Assist, Automate, Build + Integrate, or Escalate—each with a different authority, effort, and evidence profile.

03

BOUNDED FIELD TESTS

A small cross-functional portfolio tested in real work with explicit human authority, exceptions, feedback, and stop criteria.

04

CHAMPION + LEARNING CADENCE

Department advocates, office hours, reusable examples, feedback loops, and behavior reinforcement tied to workflows.

05

EVP OPERATING REVIEW

A concise readout of demand quality, flow, retained adoption, value, governed usage, blockers, and tradeoffs.

06

R&D / IT BOUNDARY CONTRACT

Clear escalation paths for product, platform, architecture, identity, access, security, and infrastructure decisions.
